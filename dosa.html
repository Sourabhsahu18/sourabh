Dosa Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup of rice (regular white rice or parboiled rice)
  • 1/4 cup of urad dal (split black gram)
  • 1/2 teaspoon of fenugreek seeds
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil or ghee for cooking
  • 1 tablespoon of chana dal (split chickpeas)

Instructions:
  1. Rinse the rice, urad dal, chana dal, and fenugreek seeds separately under cold water until the water runs clear. Soak them all together in water for at least 4-6 hours, or preferably overnight.
  2. After soaking, drain the water and add the rice and dal mixture to a blender. Add some water (about 1/2 to 3/4 cup) and blend until you get a smooth batter. The batter should have a slightly thick consistency, similar to pancake batter. Add salt to taste and mix well.
  3. Transfer the batter to a large bowl and let it ferment in a warm place for about 8-12 hours or overnight. During fermentation, the batter will rise and become slightly fluffy.
  4. Once the batter is fermented, give it a gentle stir. If it's too thick, you can add a little water to adjust the consistency.
  5. Heat a non-stick or cast-iron skillet over medium-high heat. Once hot, lightly grease the skillet with oil or ghee.
  6. Pour a ladleful of batter onto the center of the skillet and spread it in a circular motion to form a thin, even layer. You can make the dosa as thin or thick as you like, depending on your preference.
  7. Drizzle some oil or ghee around the edges of the dosa and cook for 1-2 minutes, or until the bottom turns golden brown and crisp.
  8. Flip the dosa using a spatula and cook the other side for another minute or until it's cooked through and lightly browned.
  9. Remove the dosa from the skillet and serve hot with your favorite chutney, sambar, or any other accompaniment of your choice.
  10. Repeat the process with the remaining batter, greasing the skillet lightly before making each dosa

    11. Enjoy your homemade dosas!
