Upma Recipe :



Ingredients:

  • 1 cup semolina (rava)
  • 2 tablespoons oil or ghee
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 tablespoon urad dal (split black gram lentils)
  • 1 tablespoon chana dal (split chickpea lentils)
  • 2-3 green chilies, finely chopped
  • 1 inch ginger, finely chopped
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 1 medium carrot, finely chopped (optional)
  • 1/4 cup peas (fresh or frozen, optional)
  • 2 cups water
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish (optional)
  • Lemon wedges for serving (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Dry roast the semolina in a pan on medium heat until it turns aromatic and light golden brown. Keep stirring continuously to prevent burning. Once done, transfer it to a plate and set aside.
  2. In the same pan, heat oil or ghee over medium heat. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter.
  3. Add cumin seeds, urad dal, and chana dal. Fry until the dals turn golden brown.
  4. Add chopped green chilies and ginger. Sauté for a minute until they release their aroma.
  5. Add chopped onions and cook until they turn translucent.
  6. If using, add chopped carrots and peas. Sauté for a couple of minutes until they soften slightly.
  7. Pour in 2 cups of water and add salt to taste. Bring the water to a boil.
  8. Once the water is boiling, reduce the heat to low and slowly add the roasted semolina, stirring continuously to avoid lumps.
  9. Cook the upma on low heat, stirring frequently, until it thickens and the semolina is cooked completely. It should take about 5-7 minutes.
  10. Once done, turn off the heat and let the upma rest for a couple of minutes.
  11. Garnish with freshly chopped coriander leaves if desired.
  12. Serve hot with a wedge of lemon on the side for squeezing over the upma.
Enjoy your delicious homemade upma! Feel free to adjust the spice levels and add your favorite vegetables to customize the dish to your liking.
